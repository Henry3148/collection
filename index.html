Koenigsegg Models

The Koenigsegg Agera was the successor to the CCX/CCXR. The name comes from the Swedish verb 'agera' which means "to act".

The Agera S was created for markets which do not have E85 fuel. It is basically an Agera R apart from the flexfuel capability.

The CC8S was the first ever production car from Koenigsegg.

Koenigsegg created a race car - the CCGT, based on the production CC-model range.

The CCR was an evolution of the CC8S and was produced between 2004 and 2006.

The name comes from the combination of two Swedish words, notably ge, or “to give,” and mera, “more.”

Koenigsegg's new Jesko hypercar, named after his father who helped him start his company, claims over 300 mph as its top speed.

For the 2014 Geneva Motor Show, Koenigsegg created the one:1 based on the Agera platform.

Besides a regular engine, the Koenigsegg Regera also carries an electric unit which gives out 700 horsepower and 663 lb-ft of torque with 9 kWh liquid cooled battery.
